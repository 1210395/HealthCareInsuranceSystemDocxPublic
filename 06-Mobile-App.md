# Mobile App

## What the Mobile App Does

The mobile app gives patients convenient access to their healthcare insurance information from their phone. It is designed to be simple and easy to use, focusing on the features patients need most in their daily lives.

The app is available in both Arabic and English, and patients can switch between languages at any time.


## Who Can Use It

The mobile app is for **patients and their family members**. Healthcare providers (doctors, pharmacists, etc.) and insurance company staff use the web-based system instead.

- Every insured patient can download and use the app.
- Adult family members on the same plan each get their own login.
- Children's information is accessible through the parent's account.


## Key Features

### View Medical History

Patients can see a complete record of their medical interactions:
- Past doctor visits and diagnoses
- Prescriptions (current and past)
- Lab test results
- Radiology and imaging results
- Claim history and statuses

**Important note about pricing:** When patients view their medical history through the mobile app, they see the medical details but not the prices or costs. This is by design, as the focus of the medical history view is on health information, not financial details. Cost information is available through the full insurance portal.

### QR Code for Identity Verification

The app generates a unique QR code (a square barcode image) that the patient can show at the doctor's office to instantly verify their identity and insurance coverage. (More details on how this works are described below.)

### Insurance Card Information

The app displays the patient's insurance card digitally. This includes their policy details, member ID, and coverage information. The patient always has their insurance information with them on their phone.

### Family Member Management

Patients who are primary account holders can:
- View all family members on their plan
- See each family member's medical information
- Manage children's accounts
- Add or update family member details

### Notifications

The app sends push notifications to keep patients informed in real time:
- When a claim status changes (for example, from "Under Review" to "Approved")
- When lab test results are available
- When a prescription has been dispensed
- When there is a message from insurance support
- Other important updates

Tapping a notification takes the patient directly to the relevant screen in the app.


## How QR Code Verification Works

The QR code feature replaces the need for manual identity verification at the doctor's office. Here is how it works in practice:

### Step 1: Patient Opens the App

When the patient arrives at the doctor's office, they open the mobile app on their phone.

### Step 2: Patient Shows the QR Code

The app displays the patient's personal QR code. The patient shows their phone screen to the doctor or receptionist.

### Step 3: Doctor Scans the Code

The doctor or receptionist scans the QR code using their system (a camera, scanner, or their own device connected to the system).

### Step 4: System Verifies Instantly

The system immediately:
- Confirms the patient's identity
- Displays the patient's name and photo
- Shows their insurance coverage status
- Confirms the policy is active

### Step 5: Doctor Proceeds with Treatment

With identity and coverage confirmed, the doctor can begin the consultation knowing exactly who the patient is and what their insurance covers.

### The Entire Process Takes Just a Few Seconds

```
Patient opens app  -->  Shows QR code  -->  Doctor scans  -->  Identity confirmed  -->  Treatment begins
     (2 seconds)        (1 second)          (1 second)         (instant)
```


## Why QR Codes Are Used

**Prevents fraud:** The QR code is linked to the patient's verified account. Someone cannot easily pretend to be another person to use their insurance.

**Faster than manual lookup:** Instead of the receptionist typing in a name or ID number and searching through records, one scan does everything instantly.

**Always available:** As long as the patient has their phone, they have their identification. No need to carry a separate insurance card (though physical cards are still supported as a backup).

**Secure:** The QR code is generated fresh and is tied to the patient's authenticated session in the app. It cannot be easily copied or reused by someone else.


## Insurance Card as Backup

While the QR code is the preferred method, the system still supports traditional insurance cards:

- Patients can use a physical insurance card if their phone is unavailable.
- The digital version of the card is always visible in the app.
- The card contains a barcode that must be **scanned** by the provider using a barcode reader or camera — the card number cannot be entered manually. This is a security measure to prevent unauthorized use of someone else's card number.

The insurance card serves as a reliable backup to ensure patients can always access their care.


## Family Member Accounts

### Adults

Each adult family member on a plan receives their own separate login to the mobile app. They can:
- Log in with their own credentials
- View their own medical history
- Show their own QR code at the doctor's office
- Receive their own notifications

### Children

Children do not have their own accounts. Instead:
- The parent manages the child's information through their own account.
- The parent can show the child's QR code from their own app when taking the child to the doctor.
- The parent receives notifications about the child's claims and results.
- When the child reaches adulthood, they can be given their own separate account.
