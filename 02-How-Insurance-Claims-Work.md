# How Insurance Claims Work

## What Is an Insurance Claim?

An insurance claim is a formal request for the insurance company to pay for a medical service that a patient received. When you visit a doctor, get a prescription, have a lab test done, or undergo an imaging procedure, someone needs to tell the insurance company what happened so they can cover the cost according to your policy.

Think of it like a receipt that gets sent to the insurance company, along with a request: "Please pay for this medical service."


## How a Standard Claim Works (Step by Step)

A standard claim is submitted by a healthcare provider (such as a doctor or hospital) that is part of the insurance network. Here is what happens from start to finish:

### Step 1: Patient Visits the Doctor

The patient goes to their doctor for treatment. The doctor examines the patient and provides the necessary medical care. This might include a consultation, diagnosis, treatment, prescriptions, or orders for lab tests and imaging.

### Step 2: Doctor Submits the Claim

After the visit, the doctor (or the clinic's staff) submits a claim through the system. The claim includes:
- Who the patient is
- What services were provided
- The diagnosis
- The total cost
- Any prescriptions written
- Any lab tests or imaging ordered

The system automatically calculates how much the insurance should cover and how much the patient should pay, based on the patient's insurance policy.

### Step 3: Medical Admin Reviews the Claim

A Medical Administrator at the insurance company receives the claim and reviews it from a medical perspective. They check:
- Does the treatment make sense for the diagnosis?
- Are the services appropriate?
- Is anything unusual or suspicious?

The Medical Admin can:
- **Approve** the claim (it moves to the next step)
- **Reject** the claim (if the medical justification is not sufficient)
- **Return** the claim (if more information is needed from the doctor)

### Step 4: Coordination Admin Reviews Financial Aspects

Once the Medical Admin approves, a Coordination Administrator reviews the financial side:
- Does the claim match the patient's policy?
- Are the amounts correct?
- Are there any coverage limits that apply?

The Coordination Admin can:
- **Give final approval** (the claim is ready for payment)
- **Reject** the claim (if it does not meet financial or policy requirements)
- **Return** the claim (if financial details need correction)

### Step 5: Payment Is Processed

Once the claim has final approval, the Coordination Admin processes the payment. The payment goes to the healthcare provider (the doctor or hospital).

### Step 6: Payment Is Confirmed

The payment is recorded in the system with details such as the payment method and a receipt number. Both the provider and the patient can see that the claim has been paid.

## How a Reimbursement Claim Works

Sometimes a patient visits a doctor or facility that is not part of the insurance network. In this case, the patient pays the full cost out of their own pocket at the time of the visit.

Afterward, the patient can submit a reimbursement claim to get back the portion that their insurance should have covered.

### How It Works:

1. **Patient visits an out-of-network provider** and pays the full amount themselves.
2. **Patient submits a reimbursement claim** through the system, attaching proof of payment (receipts, medical reports).
3. **Medical Admin reviews** the claim to verify the medical service was legitimate.
4. **Coordination Admin reviews** the financial details and determines how much the insurance will reimburse.
5. **The insurance company pays the patient** the covered amount.

The key difference from a standard claim: the money goes back to the patient, not to the doctor.


## What Happens When a Claim Is Returned

If either the Medical Admin or the Coordination Admin finds an issue with a claim, they do not have to reject it outright. Instead, they can **return** the claim to the doctor or provider for correction.

### The Return and Ticket Process:

1. The admin returns the claim with a note explaining what needs to be fixed.
2. A **ticket** is created automatically, recording the reason for the return.
3. The doctor or provider receives a notification that the claim has been returned.
4. The doctor fixes the issue (corrects information, adds missing details, etc.) and resubmits the claim.
5. The claim goes through the review process again.

This return process can happen more than once if needed. Every return is recorded, creating a clear history of communications about the claim.


## Emergency Claims

Emergency claims work similarly to standard claims but are flagged as emergencies. This means:
- They may receive priority in the review process.
- Different coverage rules may apply (for example, higher coverage for genuine emergencies).
- The claim includes details about the emergency nature of the visit.

The review process is the same: medical review first, then financial review, then payment.


## What Each Person Sees

### What the Patient Sees

- The current status of their claim (for example: "Under Medical Review" or "Payment Processed")
- How much the insurance will cover and how much they owe
- Notifications when the status changes
- Their complete claim history
- They do NOT see internal review notes between administrators

### What the Doctor Sees

- Claims they have submitted
- Whether claims were approved, rejected, or returned
- Return tickets with notes about what needs to be fixed
- Their patients' medical history (for treatment purposes)
- They do NOT see the financial review details or payment processing

### What the Admin Sees

- All claims that need their review
- The full history of each claim (all reviews, returns, notes)
- Financial details and policy information
- Payment records
- Statistics and reports on claims


## Claim Statuses Explained in Plain Language

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Pending Medical Review** | The claim has been submitted and is waiting for a Medical Admin to review the medical aspects. |
| **Awaiting Coordination Review** | The medical review is done. The claim is now waiting for a Coordination Admin to review the financial aspects. |
| **Approved (Medical)** | The Medical Admin has approved the claim from a medical standpoint. It still needs financial review. |
| **Rejected (Medical)** | The Medical Admin has determined that the medical justification is not sufficient. The claim will not be paid. |
| **Approved (Final)** | Both the medical and financial reviews are complete. The claim is approved and ready for payment. |
| **Rejected (Final)** | The Coordination Admin has rejected the claim for financial or policy reasons. The claim will not be paid. |
| **Returned for Review** | An admin has sent the claim back because more information or corrections are needed. |
| **Returned to Provider** | The claim has been sent back to the doctor or provider to fix and resubmit. |
| **Payment Pending** | The claim has been approved and payment is being processed. |
| **Paid** | The payment has been completed. The claim is fully settled. |

**Important note for patients:** If your claim shows "Rejected," you will receive an explanation of why. If your claim shows "Returned," it means the doctor needs to provide additional information. It is not a rejection; it is a request for clarification.
