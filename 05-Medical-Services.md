# Medical Services

## Overview

This system manages several types of medical services, each with its own workflow. This document explains how each service works from the perspective of the people involved.


## Prescriptions

### What Is a Prescription in This System?

A prescription is a digital order from a doctor for a patient to receive specific medicine. Instead of writing it on a paper pad, the doctor enters it into the system, and the pharmacist receives it electronically.

### How the Prescription Process Works

**Step 1: Doctor Writes the Prescription**
After examining the patient, the doctor creates a prescription in the system. The prescription includes:
- The medicine name
- The form of medicine (tablet, capsule, liquid, cream, injection, etc.)
- The dosage (how much to take)
- How often to take it (for example, twice a day)
- How long to take it (for example, 10 days)
- Any special instructions

For liquid medicines, the system can suggest appropriate bottle sizes based on the total amount needed. For children, the doctor can specify doses based on the child's weight.

**Step 2: Pharmacist Receives the Prescription**
The pharmacist sees the new prescription in their queue. They review it to ensure:
- The medicine and dosage are appropriate
- There are no conflicts with the patient's known allergies or other medications
- The prescription is clear and complete

**Step 3: Pharmacist Dispenses the Medicine**
Once verified, the pharmacist prepares the medicine and gives it to the patient. They mark the prescription as dispensed in the system.

**Step 4: Records Are Updated**
The prescription, including what was dispensed, becomes part of the patient's permanent medical record.

### What If There Is a Problem?

If the pharmacist notices an issue (wrong dosage, potential allergy conflict, unclear instructions), they can flag the prescription and communicate back to the doctor through the system. The doctor can then update the prescription as needed.


## Lab Tests

### What Are Lab Tests in This System?

Lab tests are laboratory examinations ordered by a doctor, such as blood tests, urine tests, or other diagnostic tests. The system tracks the entire process from the doctor's order to the final results.

### How Lab Test Requests Work

**Step 1: Doctor Orders Lab Tests**
During a patient visit, the doctor determines that lab tests are needed. They create a lab test order in the system, specifying:
- Which tests are needed
- The reason for the tests (the diagnosis being investigated)
- Any special instructions for the lab

**Step 2: Lab Technician Receives the Request**
The lab technician sees the new request in their queue. They can see what tests are needed and any relevant patient information.

**Step 3: Samples Are Collected and Tests Are Performed**
The patient provides the necessary samples (blood, urine, etc.). The lab technician processes the tests.

**Step 4: Results Are Uploaded**
Once the tests are complete, the lab technician uploads the results into the system. This may include numerical values, reference ranges (what is considered normal), and any notes.

**Step 5: Results Are Available**
Both the doctor and the patient can view the results:
- The **doctor** sees the full results and uses them for diagnosis and treatment decisions.
- The **patient** can view their results through their account or the mobile app. Note: the patient sees the medical results but not the pricing information.


## Radiology

### What Is Radiology in This System?

Radiology refers to medical imaging services such as X-rays, ultrasounds, CT scans, and MRI scans. The system tracks the process from the doctor's order to the final imaging report.

### How Radiology Requests Work

**Step 1: Doctor Orders Imaging**
The doctor determines that imaging is needed and creates a radiology order in the system, specifying:
- The type of imaging (X-ray, ultrasound, CT, MRI, etc.)
- The body part to be imaged
- The reason for the imaging
- Any special instructions

**Step 2: Radiologist Receives the Request**
The radiologist sees the new request in their queue with all the details needed to perform the procedure.

**Step 3: Imaging Is Performed**
The patient comes to the radiology facility and the imaging procedure is carried out.

**Step 4: Results and Report Are Uploaded**
The radiologist uploads the imaging results and writes a report describing what was found. This report becomes part of the patient's medical record.

**Step 5: Results Are Available**
The doctor receives the results and uses them for diagnosis. The patient can also view their imaging results through the system.


## Emergency Services

### How Emergency Claims Are Handled

Emergencies require urgent medical attention. The system accommodates this with a streamlined process:

1. **The patient receives emergency care.** Treatment comes first; paperwork comes second.
2. **The provider submits an emergency claim.** The claim is flagged as an emergency, which may qualify it for different coverage rules under the patient's policy (often higher coverage for genuine emergencies).
3. **Review process.** Emergency claims go through the same medical and financial review as standard claims, but the emergency flag is visible to reviewers.
4. **Coverage is applied.** The policy's emergency-specific coverage rules are used to calculate the insurance payment.

Emergency claims may include multiple services provided during a single emergency visit (doctor consultation, lab tests, imaging, procedures), all bundled together.


## Chronic Disease Management

### What Is Chronic Disease Management?

Chronic diseases are long-term health conditions that require ongoing care, such as diabetes, high blood pressure, asthma, or heart disease. The system helps manage these conditions over time.

### How It Works

- **Tracking:** The system maintains a record of the patient's chronic conditions, making this information available to all their healthcare providers.
- **Scheduled Visits:** Regular check-up schedules can be set up for patients with chronic conditions.
- **Medication Management:** Ongoing prescriptions for chronic conditions are tracked, making refills smoother and ensuring continuity of care.
- **History:** All visits, test results, and treatments related to the chronic condition are linked together, giving doctors a complete picture of how the condition has been managed over time.

This helps ensure that patients with chronic conditions receive consistent, well-coordinated care even if they see different doctors.


## Family Members

### How Family Coverage Works

Insurance in this system can cover more than just the primary account holder. Family members can be added to a plan, including:

- **Spouse**
- **Children**
- **Parents**
- **Siblings**

### How It Works

1. **The primary account holder** (the main insured person) registers in the system.
2. **They add family members** to their plan, providing each person's information and their relationship (spouse, child, parent, sibling).
3. **Each adult family member** gets their own login to the system and the mobile app. They can view their own medical information and manage their own appointments.
4. **Children** are managed by the parent's account. The parent can view and manage all medical information for their children.
5. **All family members** are covered under the same insurance policy, with the same coverage rules applying to everyone on the plan.

When a doctor treats a family member, the claim is linked to the correct person, and the primary account holder can see the overall family coverage usage.


## Visit Sessions (For Hospitals and Clinics)

### What Are Visit Sessions?

When a patient visits a hospital or clinic, they may see multiple providers in a single visit. For example, during one hospital visit, a patient might:
- See a doctor for a consultation
- Get blood drawn for lab tests
- Have an X-ray taken
- Pick up a prescription from the hospital pharmacy

### How Bundled Visits Work

Instead of creating separate claims for each of these services, the system allows them to be bundled into a single **visit session**.

1. **The visit begins** when the patient checks in at the organization (hospital or clinic).
2. **Multiple services** are recorded under the same visit.
3. **A single claim** (or a coordinated set of claims) is submitted for the entire visit.
4. **The patient sees one summary** of what was done and what it cost, rather than being overwhelmed by multiple separate claims.

This is particularly useful for hospitals where patients often receive several services in one trip. It simplifies the process for everyone: the patient, the hospital staff, and the insurance reviewers.
